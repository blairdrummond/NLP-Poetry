title : American Roots: Moral Associations

author : Primus St. John

text : 
1    Kinship: 
      Is embarrassing the wind, 
      Like dead black boys, 
      Falling down from the trees, 
      Then downstream– 
      On their knees, 
      Blood like, 
      Like a rich nation. 

2    Metaphor: 
      Becomes humiliating, 
      And clean, 
      Ticking like a ripe machine. 
      Do not 
      Bend, 
      Fold, 
      Or mutilate me– 
      This is your future speaking. 

3    The air smells so metaphysical 
      We have accused it– 
      Of smog, 
      And lost manhood, 
      Then all ritual. 

4    Whoever wrote: 
      A view is a mountain speaking 
      But left the introduction 
      For the snow, 
      And accused silence 
      Of its soul. 

5    The whole nation: 
      Is a stanza of blackness, 
      A huge white whale, 
      Faith in space 
      (Like the newspapers), 
      And the quiet insistence 
      We have peace, 
      And it’s your world, brother.
