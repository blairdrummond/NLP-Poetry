Fame is a fickle food (1702) 
Emily Dickinson 
1830-1886

U.S., New, England
Social Commentaries, Popular Culture



Fame is a fickle food 
Upon a shifting plate 
Whose table once a 
Guest but not 
The second time is set 
Whose crumbs the crows inspect 
And with ironic caw 
Flap past it to the 
Farmer's corn 
Men eat of it and die
